# TEMPLATE - PRODUCT MARKETING CONTEXT

**Use this template to create or refresh `contexts/product-marketing-context.md` during Project Inception (Phase 1A).**

This template is for authoring. The final runtime file should be shorter, factual, and actively maintained.

---

## WHAT TO CAPTURE

- Who the ideal customer is and what problem they are trying to solve
- The language the customer uses to describe their problem
- What makes this product different from alternatives (Unique Value Proposition)
- The core brand voice and tone guidelines
- Key proof points (numbers, claims, guarantees)

---

## AUTHORING QUESTIONS

### 1. Audience
- Who is the ideal customer?
- What specific problem are they trying to solve?
- What objections or hesitations do they typically have?
- What words or phrases do they use when complaining about the current alternatives?

### 2. Product/Offer
- What is the ONE primary transformation or outcome this product delivers?
- What are the top 3 specific benefits (not features) of the product?
- What makes it different from existing alternatives in the market?
- Do we have any proof points yet (guarantees, metrics, case studies)?

### 3. Voice and Tone
- What is the brand personality? (e.g., Bold and direct? Professional but friendly? Irreverent and fun?)
- What words should we NEVER use? (e.g., Avoid corporate jargon like 'synergy' or 'utilize').

---

## RUNTIME FILE TARGET SHAPE

When you write the actual `contexts/product-marketing-context.md`, keep it in this shape:

1. Target Audience Profile
2. Core Pain Points & Objections
3. Unique Value Proposition (UVP)
4. Key Benefits
5. Brand Voice & Tone Rules
6. Approved Proof Points

---

## FINAL RULE

The runtime `product-marketing-context.md` should read like current truth for a copywriter. It should be specific enough that someone could write a high-converting landing page without asking clarifying questions.
