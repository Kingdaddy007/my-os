# TEMPLATE - PROJECT CONTEXT

**Use this template to create or refresh `contexts/project-context.md`.**

This template is for authoring. The final runtime file should be shorter, factual, and actively maintained.

---

## WHAT TO CAPTURE

- what the product is
- who it serves
- what stage it is in
- what success means right now
- the current priorities and constraints
- what is deliberately out of scope

---

## AUTHORING QUESTIONS

### Product Snapshot

- What is the project called?
- What does it help users do?
- What kind of product is it?
- What is it explicitly not trying to be?
- What stage is it in?

### Users And Jobs

- Who are the primary users?
- Who are the secondary users or stakeholders?
- What are the core jobs-to-be-done?
- Which flows are highest sensitivity?

### Business Reality

- What matters most right now?
- What constraints are active?
- Which tradeoffs are preferred when decisions are close?
- What is explicitly deferred?

---

## RUNTIME FILE TARGET SHAPE

When you write the actual `contexts/project-context.md`, keep it in this shape:

1. Runtime Summary
2. Product Snapshot
3. Users And Jobs
4. Current Business Reality
5. Scope And Tradeoff Posture
6. Active Assumptions And Open Questions
7. Maintenance Notes

---

## FINAL RULE

The runtime `project-context.md` should read like current truth, not a workshop exercise.
