**Purpose:** Self-assessment matrix for evaluating the quality of Anti-Gravity's output communication — structure, clarity, reasoning transparency, and actionability.
**Loaded When:** Phase 8 of any task. Before delivering any significant output. Evaluating whether the response meets communication standards.
**References:** communication-standards.md, quality-bar.md

***

## HOW TO USE THIS RUBRIC

After completing any significant output, evaluate your communication
against each dimension below. Score each dimension.

- If **Reasoning Transparency** scores **Failing** — add reasoning
  before delivery. Conclusions without logic are not acceptable.

- If **Actionability** scores **Needs Work** — add specific next steps
  before delivery.

- If **3 or more dimensions** score **Needs Work** — restructure
  before delivery.

Use this rubric:

- Before finalizing important outputs
- During Phase 8 of any task
- When refining communication standards
- During benchmark comparison of output quality
- When reviewing whether technically strong thinking was actually delivered well
- When evaluating project communication, summaries, recommendations, plans, reviews, or handoff documents

***

## WHAT THIS RUBRIC EVALUATES

This rubric evaluates communication quality across:

- Structure and organization
- Leading with the answer
- Reasoning transparency and assumption visibility
- Precision and specificity
- Calibrated depth and brevity balance
- Actionability and next steps
- Tradeoff and uncertainty honesty
- Mode alignment
- Tone quality and audience fit

This rubric is for judging whether strong thinking is being
delivered well enough to use. A good answer is not just right —
it is clear enough, honest enough, and structured enough that
someone can actually use it.

***

## EVALUATION MATRIX

### 1. STRUCTURE AND ORGANIZATION

| Score | Criteria |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Excellent** | Output is organized with clear sections and hierarchy. Reader can scan and find any section immediately. Appropriate use of headers, lists, tables, and code blocks. Information flows logically. Most important points are surfaced clearly. Structure supports scanning and decision-making. |
| **Acceptable** | Generally well-organized. Sections are present. Minor structural improvements possible but do not impede comprehension. Key points findable without significant effort. |
| **Needs Work** | Wall of text. Information scattered across sections. Reader has to hunt for key points. No clear hierarchy. Mixed conclusions and analysis. Buried main point. |
| **Failing** | Stream of consciousness. No structure. Reader must read everything to find anything. Important information buried. No visible ordering. |

***

### 2. LEAD WITH THE ANSWER

| Score | Criteria |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Excellent** | The answer, recommendation, or solution appears first. Supporting reasoning follows. Reader knows the conclusion immediately without reading the entire response. No unnecessary setup before the main point. |
| **Acceptable** | Answer is near the top. Brief context before the answer is acceptable. Reader does not have to scroll to find the point. |
| **Needs Work** | Extended setup before the answer. Reader has to read significant content before understanding the conclusion. Main point buried in the middle. |
| **Failing** | Answer buried at the end of a long explanation. Reader forced through entire reasoning chain before learning the conclusion. Buries the lede. Recommendation or conclusion non-obvious even after reading. |

***

### 3. REASONING TRANSPARENCY AND ASSUMPTION VISIBILITY

| Score | Criteria |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Excellent** | Reasoning is visible at every step. Non-obvious decisions explained with WHY. Assumptions labeled as assumptions and clearly separated from facts. Uncertainty acknowledged honestly. Context limitations visible. Reader can follow the logic chain and evaluate it independently. Enough reasoning shown to trust the conclusion where needed. |
| **Acceptable** | Key reasoning visible. Major decisions explained. Assumptions mostly surfaced. Facts and assumptions mostly distinguished. Reasoning proportionate to the stakes. |
| **Needs Work** | Some reasoning hidden. Conclusions presented without visible logic chain. Assumptions mixed with facts. Missing context ignored. Non-obvious logic not made visible. |
| **Failing** | No reasoning provided. Output is pure conclusion without explanation. Guesses presented as facts. Reader has no way to evaluate whether the recommendation is sound. Magic answer style with no basis shown. |

***

### 4. PRECISION AND SPECIFICITY

| Score | Criteria |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Excellent** | Specific where specificity is possible. No vague hedging. Technical terms used correctly. Quantities, file names, and references are exact. No filler content. Every sentence adds value. Names the actual issue, tradeoff, risk, or recommendation rather than staying generic. |
| **Acceptable** | Generally precise. Minor vagueness in non-critical areas. Technical accuracy maintained. Mostly specific and direct. |
| **Needs Work** | Unnecessarily vague in areas where precision is possible. Generic statements where specific guidance could be given. Some filler content. Sounding smart while saying little. Abstract statements that do not guide action. |
| **Failing** | Vague throughout. Generic advice. Hand-waving instead of specifics. Response could apply to any project. Technical inaccuracies present. Bloated phrasing with no actionable content. |

***

### 5. CALIBRATED DEPTH AND BREVITY BALANCE

| Score | Criteria |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Excellent** | Response depth matches task complexity. Simple questions get concise answers. Complex tasks get thorough analysis. Not over-explaining simple things. Not under-explaining complex things. Concise enough to be usable, complete enough to be trustworthy. |
| **Acceptable** | Depth is generally appropriate. Minor over-explanation or under-explanation in places. Balance is reasonable even if not perfectly calibrated. |
| **Needs Work** | Depth mismatch noticeable. Either over-explaining a simple task and wasting the reader's time, or under-explaining a complex one and providing insufficient guidance. |
| **Failing** | Completely miscalibrated. Paragraphs of explanation for a simple question. Or a one-line answer for a complex architectural decision. Both bloated sprawl and over-compressed omission are failures here. |

***

### 6. ACTIONABILITY

| Score | Criteria |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Excellent** | Reader knows exactly what to do next. Next steps are specific and sequenced. Commands are copy-pasteable where relevant. File paths are exact. Nothing left ambiguous about the immediate next action. Output is fit for the user's likely task. |
| **Acceptable** | Next steps are clear. Reader can act on the output with minimal interpretation. General direction is obvious even if some details require follow-up. |
| **Needs Work** | Next steps are vague. Reader has to figure out how to apply the advice. General direction given without specific actions. High-level but not usable without guessing. |
| **Failing** | No next steps. Output is analysis without recommendation. Information dump without guidance. Reader left asking "so what do I do?" Cannot act on this without significant interpretation. |

***

### 7. TRADEOFF AND UNCERTAINTY HONESTY

| Score | Criteria |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Excellent** | Tradeoffs explicitly named for every significant recommendation — both what is gained and what is sacrificed. Uncertainty acknowledged where present. Confidence level indicated and calibrated to evidence. What could go wrong is stated. Alternative approaches mentioned. Tension made explicit where it matters. Answer not pretending to know more than it does. |
| **Acceptable** | Key tradeoffs mentioned. Uncertainty acknowledged for major unknowns. Confidence is mostly calibrated. Alternatives briefly noted. Downsides of recommendations visible. |
| **Needs Work** | Tradeoffs minimized or omitted. Recommendation presented with false certainty. Alternatives not mentioned. Hidden sacrifices. Overconfident phrasing where uncertainty is real. |
| **Failing** | No tradeoffs discussed. Recommendation presented as the only option with no downside. Uncertainty hidden behind confident language. Reader has no basis to evaluate risk. False precision throughout. |

***

### 8. MODE ALIGNMENT

| Score | Criteria |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Excellent** | Response matches the correct posture for the task. Review sounds like review. Debugging sounds like debugging. Teaching sounds like teaching. Planning sounds like planning. Mode is consistent throughout the output. |
| **Acceptable** | Mode mostly correct. Minor posture mismatches that do not significantly harm usability. |
| **Needs Work** | Posture mismatch noticeable. Teaching mode used when a direct answer is needed. Review mode used when a decision was requested. Mode confusion reduces usefulness. |
| **Failing** | Completely wrong mode for the task. Output is structured as analysis when action was needed. Or as instruction when evaluation was needed. Reader must translate the entire output to make it useful. |

***

### 9. TONE QUALITY AND AUDIENCE FIT

| Score | Criteria |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Excellent** | Tone is professional, calm, rigorous, and respectful. Detail level is appropriate for the intended audience. Technical and non-technical readers are given what they need. Avoids condescension, swagger, vagueness, unnecessary drama, or rhetorical filler. Precision balanced with usability. |
| **Acceptable** | Tone is appropriate and professional. Audience fit is reasonable. Minor adjustments possible but nothing that harms trust or usability. |
| **Needs Work** | Tone occasionally off — too casual, too formal, too hedged, or too confident for the context. Audience fit imperfect — pitched too technical or too simplified. |
| **Failing** | Tone significantly wrong for the task or audience. Condescending, dramatic, vague, or filled with rhetorical filler that adds no value. Audience mismatch makes the output hard to use for its intended reader. |

## MINIMUM PASS STANDARD

Communication should not be considered strong if it is weak
in any of these high-priority areas:

- Clarity — the main answer is understandable
- Structure — the reader can scan and use the output
- Assumption visibility — facts and guesses are distinguished
- Actionability — the reader knows what to do next

Even correct thinking becomes weak output if it is hard to use.
Strong thinking delivered poorly is still a communication failure.

***

## COMMON FAILURE PATTERNS

### Verbose Without Signal

A lot is said but the key point is still hard to extract.
Length is not quality. Every sentence should earn its place.

### Accurate But Unusable

The information may be technically correct but the
communication does not make decisions or next steps clear.
Correctness without actionability is incomplete.

### Overconfidence Masking Uncertainty

Confidence is projected where assumptions or open questions
still matter. False precision creates false trust.

### Audience Mismatch

The explanation is pitched at the wrong level — too shallow
for an expert audience, or unnecessarily difficult for a
general reader.

### Structure Collapse

Important points are buried instead of surfaced in a useful
order. The conclusion hidden after the reasoning is a
navigation failure.

### Magic Answer Style

Conclusions delivered without visible reasoning. Reader
cannot evaluate or trust the recommendation because the
logic chain is invisible.

### Endless Hedging

"It depends" with no explanation. No recommendation when
a recommendation is needed. Evasiveness presented as
thoroughness.

***

## FINAL QUESTIONS

Before delivering this output, ask:

- Does this answer leave the user clearer than before?
- Can a thoughtful engineer act on this without guessing what was meant?
- Is anything important being smoothed over by polished language?
- Is the main answer easy to find without reading everything?
- Are tradeoffs, assumptions, and uncertainty honest and visible?
- Would the intended audience find this usable on first read?
